Roland-Garros

Paris, France

Roland-Garros, aka The French Open, is the second Grand Slam on the tennis calendar. The tournament is played in May of each year for two weeks. It is named after World War I fighter pilot, Eugene Adrien Roland Georges Garros. In 1928 the tennis stadium was named after him to honor his legacy.

Roland-Garros is the only grand slam that is played on a clay court. The clay court provides a large contrast, and for some players is much harder than playing on a hard court. On a clay court, the ball bounces higher and slower compared to a hard court, and obviously players will slide a lot more compared to a firmer surface. As mentioned before, this can be good for some players and bad for others, but no one excels on clay like the one and only Rafael Nadal, aka The King of the Clay.

The King of the Clay

Rafa Nadal, a top ranked tennis pro throughout his decorated career, has been hailed one of the best to ever play on clay courts. He currently hold the record for most singles wins at Roland-Garros, winning the Grand Slam 12 times so far. The gold medal Spanish Olympian, is so good on clay in part due to his athleticism, and due to the ball moving slower he is able to deliever a powerful shot.
